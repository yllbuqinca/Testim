Prishtina

Newborn Monument: This is a symbol of the independence of Kosovo. The
monument is often repainted with different messages and artwork, reflecting current
events and the city's dynamic atmosphere.

Mother Teresa Square: Named after Mother Teresa, this square is a central
gathering place and home to the Mother Teresa statue.

Skanderbeg Square: This is the main square of Prishtina, where you can find
the Kosovo Government building and the National Library.

The Great Hamam: This Ottoman-era building has been renovated and now serves
as a cultural center and gallery.

Fatih Mosque: An Ottoman-era mosque located near the Great Hamam, it is one
of the city's oldest religious buildings.

Kosovo Museum: This museum provides insights into the history and culture of
Kosovo, including artifacts from different periods.

Bear Sanctuary: If you're interested in wildlife conservation, consider visiting
the Bear Sanctuary near Mramor village, which is
not far from Prishtina.

Gračanica Monastery: Although a bit outside Prishtina, this UNESCO World Heritage
site is worth a visit. It's a medieval Serbian
Orthodox monastery known for its beautiful frescoes.

Ethnological Museum: Located in a 1764 Ottoman-era building, this museum showcases
traditional Kosovo life, culture, and history.

Liburnia Film Festival: If you visit during the summer, check if the Liburnia Film
Festival is taking place. It's an annual event
that features film screenings, workshops, and discussions.

Bazaar of Prishtina (Çarshia e Vjetër): Explore the old bazaar area for a taste of
local life, traditional crafts, and street food.

Art Galleries and Cafes: Prishtina has a lively arts scene, and you can explore
various art galleries and cafes that often feature
local artists and musicians.

Remember to verify the current status and accessibility of these places, as conditions
may change.
Enjoy your visit to Prishtina!
