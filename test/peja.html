Peja

Patriarchate of Peć (Pećka Patrijaršija): A UNESCO World Heritage site, this
complex includes several medieval Serbian Orthodox monasteries, such as the Church of the
Holy Apostles, the Church of St. Demetrius, and the Church of St. Nicholas.

Bazaar of Peja (Çarshia e Pejës): Explore the old bazaar area for traditional
crafts, local products, and a taste of the city's culture.

Rugova Gorge (Lumbardhi i Pejës): A stunning natural attraction, Rugova Gorge
offers opportunities for hiking and enjoying the picturesque
landscapes. The Rugova Valley is particularly known for its beauty.

Brezovica Ski Resort: Located in the Sharr Mountains, this ski resort is not
far from Peja and is a popular destination for winter sports enthusiasts.

Bogë Village: A charming mountain village near Peja, Bogë offers a peaceful
retreat with opportunities for hiking and enjoying nature.

The Monument to Brotherhood and Unity: A historical monument built during the
Yugoslav era, located in the city center.

City Center: Explore the city center of Peja for local cafes, shops, and restaurants.
The atmosphere is lively, and you can experience the daily life of the residents.

The Lead Hamam (Hamami i Plumbit): An Ottoman-era hamam that has
been preserved and is now a cultural heritage site.

The Bajrakli Mosque: An Ottoman-era mosque located in the city center, adding to
the historical and cultural richness of Peja.
